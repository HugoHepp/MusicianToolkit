Modes

Need new flavor for your song?
Composing with modes brings
new horizons to your music.
Here is an oversimplified use of modes:

I Ionian :

Sequence: W–W–H–W–W–W–H
Major mode : Known as the Major Scale, the most used mode!

II Dorian :

Sequence: W–H–W–W–W–H–W
Minor mode : Sounds great for medieval and celtic music but is also commonly used in rock, jazz and funk.

III Phrygian :

Sequence: H–W–W–W–H–W–W
Minor mode : This mode is often perceived as Hispanic/Egyptian sounds

IV Lydian :

Sequence: W–W–W–H–W–W–H
Major mode : This mode sounds like the Ionian mode but its sharp four gives it its color, it is used in jazz and film music.

V Mixolydian :

Sequence: W–W–H–W–W–H–W
Major mode : due to its seventh flat, this mode works very well over dominant chord. It is widely used in blues, rock and funk.

VI Eolian :

Sequence: W–H–W–W–H–W–W
Minor mode : Also known as natural minor scale. It can brings a sad and melancholy color to the pieces.

VII Locrian :

Sequence: H–W–W–H–W–W–W
Minor mode : Due to its triton interval between I and V degrees, the Locrien mode is not the easiest to use.
